- Designed an interactive human–robot collaboration experiment in which a human operator and a robot jointly navigate to one of three possible target endpoints. The robot has full knowledge of the correct goal from the start, while the human does not, creating an information asymmetry that requires effective robot-to-human signaling.
- Developed and implemented signaling strategies that allow the robot to guide human partners toward the correct destination.
- Conducted controlled trials where each participant interacts with two robot conditions: a fixed-policy agent and a learning-based agent capable of adapting signaling behavior in real time.
- Compared human–robot team performance across policy types to evaluate how adaptive AI signaling improves shared task outcomes and enhances human understanding of robot intent.
- Contributed to broader Human-Centered AI objectives by examining how intelligent systems can support intuitive, cooperative, and interpretable interactions for non-expert users.
